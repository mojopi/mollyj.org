Exploring Shorelines

I'm interested in using photography and gif-making to improve my obervations and investigations of shoreline invertebrates (e.g. crabs, horseshoe crabs). Like

Similar to how Edweard Muybirdge used early movie-making technology to study locomotion (e.g. the gaits of a horse) and biomechanics, I'd like to use gifs in a similar way. I think they are particularly well-suited to observing and documenting invertebrates because it breaks movement that may seem quite foreign and subtle do us into multiple parts and allows us to see it better.

I want to know how they retract their limbs, potrude their eyes, go into their shells. I'm also interested in what triggers different behaviors and how they choose their shells.

After one photo shoot, I plan on researching the following questions and writing about them.

  • How are hermit crabs attached to their shell and how do they retract into it so quickly?
  • How do their eyes work and how do they retract and protrude the way they do?
  • What do they eat and how are they adapted to eat it?
  • Why do hermit crabs have iridescent claws?

After researching hermit crab anatomy, biomechanics, and morphology, I'd like to make robot/puppet horshoecrabs who move and sense in similar ways to the creatures I observed.
